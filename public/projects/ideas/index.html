Ideas

Here are some personal projects I am interested in working on, in no particular order:
  • The Weirdest Things
    • Analyze content tags for front-page news stories of major national newspapers to find the most unusual stories
    • See how or if the subject matter has changed over a period of several years
  • Sans Swagger
    • Use a neural network to create a rap lyric generator
  • Applications of agent-based modeling in financial portfolio analysis
  • Segregation study
    • Use US Census data to rate tracts according to levels of ethnic and racial diversity
    • As well as incomes, noting in Portland MSA the possible impact of professional athletes’ salaries on mean incomes of blacks. Professional sports teams player payrolls are public.
    • Create an interactive map to visualize findings
  • Analysis of software terms of use
    • Automated to incorporate textual changes as they are made
  • Analysis of minutes of public government meetings
    • Presenting analysis in a way that allows portions of it to be “plugged-in” to any type of public meeting where minutes are taken
  • Trump tweet generator
    • analysis of all Trump tweets
      • using Twitter API
    • use a predictive model, based on the headlines on Fox News’s website from the previous 24 hours, and check against latest tweets.
    • Shiny app with button and output textbox (so that the app doesn’t need to be running all the time)
      • display most current tweet along with what the model predicts
  • CLI app for generating random password (using R)
